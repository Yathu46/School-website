Ghandi Peace Prize for the year 2002
Got Ghandi Peace Prize for the year 2002

The President Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam presents Gandhi Peace Prize for 2002 to Bharatiya Vidya Bhavan at a function in New Delhi on March 18, 2003 (Tuesday). The former President Shri R. Venkataraman receives the award on behalf of Bhartiya Vidya Bhavan. The Prime Minister Shri Atal Bihari Vajpayee also attends the function.

Rajiv Gandhi Award For National Integration (1992)
Rajiv Gandhi Award For National Integration (1992)

The National Advisory Board of Priyadarshni Academy, a voluntary socio-cultural organization devoted to the promotion of social, cultural, educational and other activities, has selected Bharatiya Vidya Bhavan for Priyadarshni Academy's RAJIV GANDHI MEMORIAL AWARD FOR NATIONAL INTEGRATION- cash prize Rs. 1 Lakh & Trophy - for its outstanding contribution to National Integration, particularly through its Sarva Dharma Maitri Pratishthan. This Award is given by the Academy once in three years.

Bhavan's Ramakrisha Vidyalaya clinches the Tournament Trophy
Bhavan's Ramakrisha Vidyalaya clinches the Tournament Trophy

Dr. K. M. Munshi Memorial Cricket Tournament was organized on December 17th to 21, 2016. The tournament was inaugurated by Shri S. Gopalakrishnan, Chairman, School Managing Committee. Bhavan's Shri Ramakrishna Vidyalaya bagged the winners trophy. Bhavan's Public School, BHEL township and Bharatiya Vidya Bhavan's Public School, Jubilee Hills, received the first and second runner up trophies. Participants also received individual prizes for exhibiting exceptional skills in bowling, batting, wicket keeping and fielding.

  • Lecture on 'Indian Culture and Glory of Women' by Dr. Hitesh Pandya

    The Bhavan's Smt. Rasikaben Mehta (Mombasa) Memorial Lecture (in Gujarati) on 'Indian Culture and Glory of Women' was delivered online by Dr. Hitesh Pandya, Asst. Professor and Head, Dept. of Gujarati, Shri M. D. Shah Mahila College, Malad (West), Mumbai; on Thursday, 18-3-2021. Dr. Girish B. Jani, Addl. Director, Bhavan's P. G. and Research Dept., rendered the Vedic prayer and delivered the welcome address. Smt. Renu Bharat Mehta (from London) paid homage to her mother-in-law Late Smt. Rasikaben Mehta, and appreciated her selfless services and noble life. Shri Hitesh Trivedi (Sahityacharya), Assistant, Bhavan's P. G. and Research Dept. introduced the speaker Dr. Pandya; and compered the programme of the lecture. In his interesting lecture, Dr. Hitesh Pandya spoke on the importance of multi-dimensional role and place of women in human life, and glory of women as recorded in the literary and cultural heritage of India. He profusely quoted from the Vedic, Upanishadic and Puranic texts, the epics, the classical Sanskrit literature; and works of Chanakya, Adi Shankaracharya, and many saint-poets for his scholarly exposition of the woman's praise-worthy role as a daughter, sister, wife, mother, family-member and member of the society. He elaborated on the invaluable contribution of women in rearing the children, in their education, and in taking care of entire family. He also paid tributes to the select leading great women of India who, through their noble contribution, enriched the fields of learning, governance, social service, and nation-building. Ms. Foram Joshi, Guest Asst. Professor (English), Bhavan's Mumbadevi Adarsh Sanskrit Mahavidyalaya, proposed the vote of thanks.
